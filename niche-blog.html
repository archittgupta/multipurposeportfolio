The Death of Niche: Why I Refused to Choose One Path

The world tells us to specialize. From the moment we enter university, we are asked to pick a lane. Engineer? Artist? Musician? Pick one, they say, or you'll be mediocre at all of them.

For years, I tried to suppress my other interests. When I was coding embedded systems for my robotic arm project, I felt guilty for wanting to open FL Studio. When I was mixing a rap track, I felt like I was wasting time that could be spent studying VLSI design.

The Convergence Point

But then I realized something: the most interesting things happen at the intersection. My understanding of signal processing in engineering made me a better music producer. My design skills allowed me to present my technical research in ways that actually made people care.

This website is the result of that realization. It is not just a portfolio; it is a rejection of the binary. It is proof that you can be a builder and a storyteller.
